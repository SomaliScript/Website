Somali History Lesson

Somalia has a long, complex history. Below is a chronological timeline of key events that shaped the country. Scroll to explore the centuries of changes, influences, and major turning points.

A Chronological Timeline of Key Events

  • Horn of Africa Home to Somalis

    The Horn of Africa has been inhabited by Somalis since ancient times, developing a pastoral and trading culture long before external influences.

  • Ajuran Sultanate

    The powerful Ajuran Sultanate dominated much of the Horn of Africa, overseeing flourishing trade and Islamic scholarship, before fragmenting into smaller sultanates.

  • Egypt Occupies Coastal Towns

    Egypt occupies towns on the Somali coast and parts of the interior, beginning a period of increased foreign intervention.

  • Britain Proclaims Protectorate

    Britain proclaims a protectorate over Somaliland, while France and Italy also establish footholds along the coast, shaping future boundaries.

  • Independence & Formation of the Republic

    British and Italian Somalilands unite to form the independent Republic of Somalia. Aden Abdullah Osman Daar becomes the first president.

  • Ogaden War

    Somalia invades the Somali-inhabited Ogaden region of Ethiopia. After initial successes, Somali forces are pushed out by Ethiopian troops backed by Soviet and Cuban support.

  • Fall of Siad Barre, Civil Strife

    Mohamed Siad Barre is ousted, sparking a power struggle among clan warlords. Somaliland declares independence unilaterally.

  • Rise of Islamic Courts & Ethiopian Intervention

    Union of Islamic Courts gains control in Mogadishu; Ethiopia enters to support the transitional government. Al-Shabab emerges as a key insurgent group.

  • Federal Government & Renewed Hope

    First formal parliament in over 20 years is sworn in. Hassan Sheikh Mohamud is elected president, marking a new phase in Somalia's reconstruction.

  • Mohamed Abdullahi "Farmajo" Elected

    Former Prime Minister Mohamed Abdullahi Mohamed (Farmajo) wins the presidency. Al-Shabab continues to threaten stability, but the government seeks international support.
