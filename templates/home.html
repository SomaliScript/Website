Welcome to Somalia!

Somalia, officially the Federal Republic of Somalia, is a country in the Horn of Africa. It boasts the longest coastline on the African mainland, bordering the Gulf of Aden to the north and the Indian Ocean to the east. With a rich history, vibrant culture, and resilient people, Somalia is a nation of proud heritage.

This website provides an introduction to Somali culture, history, landmarks, and cuisine. Use the navigation bar above to explore each section in detail. Whether you're curious about the ancient rock art of Laas Geel or eager to discover mouth-watering Somali dishes, we've got you covered!

Geography & Climate

Somalia’s terrain consists mainly of plateaus, plains, and highlands. Temperatures are typically hot year-round, and rainfall is minimal outside of the rainy seasons. The Somali Current in the Indian Ocean influences the climate along the coast, contributing to distinct ecological zones and abundant marine life.

People & Culture

Somalis share a common language (Somali) and most are Sunni Muslims. Poetry, storytelling, and oral traditions hold a special place in Somali culture, with a rich heritage of proverbs, folk tales, and epic poetry. Somali nomadic culture has shaped values of hospitality, resilience, and strong clan relationships.

Economy & Livelihood

Traditionally, Somalia’s economy has been centered around livestock, remittances, and agriculture. Many Somalis engage in pastoralism, raising camels, goats, and sheep. In urban areas, entrepreneurship and trade are key economic drivers, with bustling markets offering a variety of goods. Remittances from the Somali diaspora worldwide also play a major role in supporting local development and livelihoods.
